How to Transfer a Car Title — Complete Guide for Buyers & Sellers

Complete checklist for buyers and sellers — avoid the most common title transfer mistakes.

For Buyers

  • Verify the seller's name matches the title exactly
  • Check for liens — the title should say "None" or have a lien release
  • Get a signed bill of sale with date, price, VIN, and both signatures
  • Complete title transfer at your local DMV within 10–30 days
  • Get the vehicle inspected/smog checked before transfer (if required)

For Sellers

  • Sign the title in the correct spot (ask DMV if unsure)
  • Fill in the odometer reading accurately — it's federal law
  • Remove your license plates (required in most states)
  • Notify your DMV of the sale (release of liability)
  • Cancel your insurance on the sold vehicle

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Buying without a title — never do this
  • Not checking for liens — you could owe the seller's bank
  • Forgetting to transfer within the deadline — late fees apply
  • Not getting a bill of sale — you have no proof of purchase
