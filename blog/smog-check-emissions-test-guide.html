Smog Check & Emissions Test Guide — All States

Which states require smog checks, how to prepare, and what to do if your car fails.

States That Require Smog/Emissions Tests

California, Texas, Arizona, Colorado, Connecticut, Delaware, Georgia, Idaho, Illinois, Indiana, Louisiana, Maine, Maryland, Massachusetts, Missouri, Nevada, New Hampshire, New Jersey, New Mexico, New York, North Carolina, Ohio, Oregon, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island, Tennessee, Utah, Vermont, Virginia, Washington, Wisconsin

What Gets Tested

  • Tailpipe emissions (CO, HC, NOx levels)
  • OBD-II diagnostic scan (1996+ vehicles)
  • Visual inspection of emissions equipment
  • Gas cap pressure test

How to Prepare

  • Drive your car for 20+ minutes before the test
  • Make sure your check engine light is off
  • Change oil and air filter if due
  • Fix any exhaust leaks
  • Use fuel system cleaner 1 week before test

What If You Fail?

You'll get a list of required repairs. Many states offer financial assistance programs for low-income owners ($500–$1,500 toward repairs). After repairs, you get a free retest within 30–60 days.
