DMV Road Test — How to Pass on Your First Try

What to expect during your behind-the-wheel test, common failure points, and expert tips.

What the Examiner Tests

  • Vehicle control: steering, braking, accelerating smoothly
  • Turns: signaling, lane position, speed control
  • Lane changes: mirror checks, blind spot, signaling
  • Parking: parallel, perpendicular, and/or angle parking
  • Intersections: right-of-way, stop signs, traffic signals
  • Highway merging (in some states)

Top Reasons People Fail

  • #1: Not checking mirrors and blind spots enough
  • #2: Rolling stops at stop signs
  • #3: Speeding — even 1 mph over can be marked
  • #4: Wide turns or turning into wrong lane
  • #5: Not yielding to pedestrians

Day-of Tips

  • Arrive 15 minutes early — late = automatic reschedule
  • Bring a clean, registered, insured vehicle with working lights
  • Adjust mirrors before examiner gets in
  • Narrate your actions ("checking mirrors, signaling left")
  • Drive slightly under the speed limit — never over
