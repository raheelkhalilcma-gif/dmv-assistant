Vehicle Registration Renewal Guide

How to renew your vehicle registration in all 50 US states — online options, fees, smog requirements, and late penalties explained step by step.

When to Renew

Most states require annual vehicle registration renewal. You should receive a renewal notice 60 days before your expiry date — but don't rely on postal mail. Use DMV Assistant to set an automated reminder and never get caught with an expired registration.

Online Renewal (Most States)

You can renew your vehicle registration online at your state DMV website if:

  • Your smog/emissions test is current (if required in your state)
  • No outstanding violations or unpaid tickets
  • A valid payment method is available

California, Texas, Florida, and New York all offer online registration renewal for most vehicles.

Smog Check States

Before you can renew your registration, certain states require a smog or emissions test:

  • Required states include: California, Texas, Arizona, Colorado, Illinois, Georgia, and 15+ others
  • Smog check must be completed before you can renew registration
  • Cost ranges from $30–$90 at certified smog check stations
